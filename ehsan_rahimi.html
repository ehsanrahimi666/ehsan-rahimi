About me

I am an environmental scientist and postdoctoral researcher at Gyeongkuk National University, South Korea, studying how climate, biodiversity, land use, and landscape structure shape ecological communities and ecosystem functioning across local to global scales.

My research integrates field ecology, remote sensing, geospatial analysis, ecological modeling, and species distribution modeling to address fundamental and applied questions in biodiversity conservation, pollination ecology, landscape ecology, and sustainable agricultural systems.

A central focus of my work is understanding how environmental change influences species interactions—particularly plant–pollinator relationships—and the ecosystem services that support food production and human well-being.

My current research integrates drone-based remote sensing, computer vision, and artificial intelligence to detect, map, and quantify flowering resources in forest and grassland ecosystems.
